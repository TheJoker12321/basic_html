Eyal Golan

Golan, who is considered a pioneer in Mizrahi music, gained fame in 1997 after releasing his third album, "Without You," produced by Tamir Kaliski and Ze'ev Nechama from Ethnix. With sales of nearly 400,000 copies, the album became the best-selling album in Israel ever. That same year, Golan became the first singer from this genre to win the title of Singer of the Year in the annual Hebrew Songs Chart of Reshet Gimmel, and his song "Tears" became the first Mizrahi song to win the title of Song of the Year. Golan's songs and albums enjoyed commercial success in both the first and second decades of the 21st century. He sold over three million albums in Israel, and in 2012 he was the most financially successful singer in the country. He won the title of "Singer of the Year" fifteen times, "Song of the Year" twelve times, "Singer of the Decade" twice, and the title of "The Singer of the State" in honor of Israel's 70th anniversary celebrations. Golan was a football player in Liga Alef for Hapoel Marmorek from Rehovot. In June 2014, he and his business partners purchased ownership of the club.

Omer Adam

Adam was born in North Carolina, United States, to Yaniv and Sharon. At the age of three, he returned to Israel with his family. He lived in the Moshav Mishmar HaShiva until 2017. In 2005, he participated in the song competition 'A Song is Born,' singing alongside Tal Mosseri the song 'Wonder Child.' His family is of Caucasian descent on his father's side and Ashkenazi on his mother's side. His father, Lt. Col. Yaniv Adam, served as deputy commander of Unit Sledge and deputy battalion commander of the 202nd Paratroopers Battalion. His maternal grandmother is the artist Aviva Shamar, and his grandfather is Brigadier General (Res.) Shmuel Adam, who is a cousin of Major General Yekutiel Adam. Another relative is Yekutiel Rabaev, who fell defending Petah Tikva as a guard of the moshav in 1917. His brothers are the singers Roy and Gal Adam. Adam served at Bahad 20 as a soldier in the Ordnance Corps.

Osher Cohen

Cohen was born and raised in Kiryat Bialik to the Hebrew singer Ilan Cohen and grew up in a musically traditional family. At the age of five, he began studying at the Kiryat Bialik Conservatory. As part of his music studies, he learned to play a variety of instruments: piano, viola, guitar, clarinet, and violin. At the age of 11, he began performing as a keyboardist in live performances. In 2018, he released his debut album "Remembered in Her Eyes." In September of that year, he released the single "In the Moments You're Leaving," which received extensive airplay on radio stations, reached second place on Media Forest's weekly chart, and achieved great success in the annual song charts. In October, he wrote and composed the song "Happy Without Me" for Omer Adam. The song was co-written with Loren Cohen and produced and arranged by Tamir Tzur. In December, the song "Inside My Head," which Cohen wrote and composed for Kobi Peretz, was released. That same year, the song "I'll Manage Alone" by Eyal Golan, which Cohen co-wrote and composed with Tamir Tzur, was also released. In March 2019, he released the song "24 Hours" together with singer Dudu Aharon and DJ Vivo. That year, he also released the song "What Does He Know" with the band Ultras, and the song "What Are You Doing to Me" with Omri Segal. In August of that year, he released the song "I Hate You," and in November, "Cipralex."
